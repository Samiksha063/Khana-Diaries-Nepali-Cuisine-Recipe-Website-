Chowmein

July 22, 2025

Chowmein is one of the most beloved and widely consumed street foods in Nepal, found everywhere from roadside stalls to fancy restaurants. Inspired by Chinese cuisine but adapted to local tastes, Nepali-style Chowmein stands out for its perfect balance of savory, spicy, and slightly tangy flavors. The dish typically features stir-fried noodles tossed with a colorful medley of vegetables like carrots, cabbage, and bell peppers, all cooked on high flame to retain a crunchy texture and smoky aroma.

Prep Time

15 mins

Cook Time

15 - 20 mins

Ingredients:

  • 200g noodles
  • 2 tablespoons oil (vegetable or sunflower)
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 tablespoon garlic, finely chopped
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, finely chopped
  • 1 medium carrot
  • 1/2 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/2 capsicum (green bell pepper), thinly sliced
  • 1 green chili (optional), chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon tomato ketchup
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar (white or apple cider)
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Spring onions for garnish

Instructions:

Boil the noodles

  1. Cook noodles in boiling water with a pinch of salt until just soft.
  2. Drain and rinse with cold water. Toss with a little oil to prevent sticking.

Prepare vegetables

  1. Slice all vegetables thinly for quick stir-frying.

Stir-fry aromatics

  1. Heat oil in a large pan or wok over medium-high heat.
  2. Add garlic, ginger, and green chili. Stir for a few seconds until fragrant.

Cook the vegetables:

  1. Add onions, carrots, cabbage, and capsicum.
  2. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes. Keep them slightly crunchy.

Add noodles and sauces:

  1. Add the boiled noodles to the pan.
  2. Pour in soy sauce, ketchup, vinegar, salt, and pepper.
  3. Toss everything well using tongs or two spoons to evenly coat the noodles.

Garnish and serve

  1. Turn off the heat. Garnish with chopped spring onions.Serve hot!

Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook noodles: Keep them slightly firm to avoid mushy texture during stir-frying.
  • Add protein: You can add scrambled eggs, paneer, or chicken for extra protein.
  • For spicy lovers: Add chili sauce or crushed chili flakes for a kick.
Happy Cooking!
