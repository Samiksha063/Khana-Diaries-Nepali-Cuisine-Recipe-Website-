Chukauni

July 22, 2025

Chukuni is a traditional and flavorful side dish that originates from the beautiful hilly regions of western Nepal, especially from Palpa, Syangja, and nearby areas. It is a humble yet delicious combination of boiled potatoes, fresh curd (dahi), and a vibrant mustard oil tempering infused with garlic, green chilies, and turmeric. Despite its simplicity, chukuni stands out for its bold and refreshing tasteâ€”a balance of creamy, tangy, spicy, and slightly pungent flavors. This dish is a staple in many rural and urban households in the west, often enjoyed during festive gatherings, family meals, or picnics. It pairs exceptionally well with chiura (beaten rice), sel roti, or roti, and serves as a cooling contrast to heavier or spicier main dishes like meat curries. Chukuni not only showcases the richness of local ingredients like mustard oil and curd but also reflects the resourceful and flavor-loving nature of Nepali home cooking.

Prep Time

10 mins

Cook Time

10 mins

Ingredients:

  • Fresh spinach (washed and chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons mustard oil (for authentic flavor)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  • 2-3 green chilies (whole or sliced, to taste)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. 3-4 medium-sized potatoes (boiled and peeled)
  2. 1 cup plain curd (dahi/yogurt)
  3. 2-3 green chilies (finely chopped)
  4. 1 tablespoon garlic (crushed or minced)
  5. 2 tablespoons mustard oil
  6. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  7. 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi)
  8. Salt to taste
  9. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook. Keep saag fresh and green.
  • A pinch of turmeric or timur can be added for variety.
Happy Cooking!
