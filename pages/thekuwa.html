Thekuwa

July 22, 2025

Thekuwa is a deep-fried sweet treat made from wheat flour, jaggery (or sugar), and ghee, often flavored with cardamom and dry coconut. It's most famously prepared during Chhath Puja and other religious festivals in the Terai region of Nepal. Crisp on the outside and slightly soft within, Thekuwa is a flavorful, long-lasting dessert that represents the richness of Madhesi culture.

Prep Time

20 mins

Cook Time

25 mins

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups wheat flour (atta)
  • 3/4 cup jaggery
  • 2-3 tablespoons grated dry coconut
  • 2 tablespoons ghee
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/2 cup warm water (as needed to make dough)
  • Ghee or oil for deep frying

Instructions:

Prepare jaggery syrup (if using jaggery)

  1. In a large mixing bowl, add wheat flour, cardamom powder, coconut, fennel seeds, and ghee.
  2. Mix until the flour resembles breadcrumbs.
  3. Gradually add jaggery syrup (or sugar water) and knead into a stiff dough.
  4. Let the dough rest for 10-15 minutes.

Shape the Thekuwa

  1. Divide the dough into small balls and flatten them into discs.
  2. Use a wooden mold, fork, or knife to create decorative patterns (optional but traditional).

Deep fry

  1. Heat oil or ghee in a deep pan on medium heat.
  2. Fry the Thekuwas in batches until they turn golden brown and crisp.
  3. Remove and drain on paper towels.

Cool and store

  1. Let them cool completely before storing in an airtight container.

Pro Tips

  • Use jaggery for authentic flavor and color.
  • Dough should be firm to hold shape while frying.
  • Fry on medium-low heat to cook evenly and avoid burning.
Happy Cooking!
