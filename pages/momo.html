Chicken Mo:Mo(Dumplings)

June 15, 2025

Momo(Dumplings) is one of the popular dish among Nepali people. It is prepared from small pieces of dough(usually All Purpose Flour) and filled with seasoned meats or vegetables and often wrapped with beautiful designs and then either steamed or fried. It is often served with spicy achar(tangy tomato pickle) which adds cherry on the top of Momo. This makes Momo soft on the outside and juicy inside.

Prep Time

45 - 60 mins

Cook Time

15 mins

Ingredients:

Momo Wrapper

  • All purpose flour
  • Lukewarm water

Momo Fillings

  • Chicken mince(Keema)
  • Coriander
  • Ginger garlic paste
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Vegetable Oil
  • Green Chillies
  • Finely chopped onions
  • Chilli flakes
  • Cumin
  • Momo masala

Instructions:

Make the dough

  1. In a bowl, add flour and slowly mix in warm water a little at a time.
  2. Knead until the dough is soft but not sticky.
  3. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 10-15 minutes.

Prepare the fillings

  1. While the dough rests, place minced meat (or veggies) in a mixing bowl.
  2. Add all filling ingredients. Mix cornflour with cold water before adding.
  3. Mix everything well using your hands or a spoon.

Make the wrappers

  1. Lightly flour a clean surface.
  2. Knead the rested dough for a minute, divide into 4 pieces.
  3. Roll each piece into a long rope, then flatten with a rolling pin.
  4. Use a cup or glass to cut out round wrappers.
  5. Dust with flour and cover with a cloth to keep soft.

Fill and shape the momo

  1. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of a wrapper.
  2. Fold and seal using pleats, or simply press into a half-moon shape.
  3. Repeat for all wrappers.

Steam the momo

  1. Grease the steamer trays with oil.
  2. Place momos in the steamer without crowding.
  3. Steam for 15 minutes until cooked.

Serve and enjoy

  1. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce or tomato achar.

Pro Tips

  • Roll wrappers thin and even for best texture.
  • Lightly oil the steamer tray to prevent sticking.
  • Don't overcrowd the steamer. Steam in batches if needed.
Happy Cooking!
